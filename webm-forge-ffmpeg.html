// CRF explained — CRF is a quality target, not a fixed bitrate. FFmpeg gives complex scenes more bits and simple ones fewer — always efficient. CRF 24 is the recommended sweet spot. CRF 0 = lossless. CRF 63 = maximum compression. Rule of thumb: go 2 steps higher for web delivery, 2 steps lower for archival.
